Who Makes the Most and Least Money Relative to Industry in New York?

Bureau of Labour Statistics data shows who makes the most and least money in industries across New York City, Newark and Jersey City.

Chief Executives make the most money in an industry with fewer jobs available.

By Shehzil Zahid Shehzil Zahid
Published APril 12, 2020 8:00 pm

The Bureau of Labor Statistics defined location quotient as “the ratio of the area concentration of occupational employment to the national average concentration”. Essentially, a location quotient greater than one indicates the occupation has a higher share of employment than average, and a location quotient less than one indicates the occupation is less common in the area than average.

Theoretically, industries with a higher occupational employment should offer lower annual mean wages given competition and industries with a lower occupational employment should offer higher annual mean wages.

The highest annual mean wage of $250, 000 belongs to “chief executives”, which have a low location quotient of 0.5.

This is a scatterplot of the correlation between location quotient and annual mean wage of occupations in New York City, Newark and Jersey City.

“Shampooers” have the lowest mean annual wage, close to $25, 000 and a location quotient of 2.4.

Jobs like “advertising and promotions managers” have a very high location quotient, between 4.5 and 5, and very high annual mean wages, close to $200, 000. Ideally, this makes “advertising and promotions managers” the likeliest career to pursue in New York City, Newark and Jersey City.

Similarly, “law teachers, post-secondary” and “financial examiners” also have high annual mean wages and location quotients, making them ideal careers to pursue in these cities.
